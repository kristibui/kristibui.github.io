about

My name is Kristi and I am a fourth year at Northeastern University on a 5-year track currently pursuing a BS in Computer Science. I love the combination of design and problem solving associated with programming, and am passionate about promoting STEM education to underrepresented minorities and exploring the intersection of technology and civics.

Currently, I am a Teaching Assistant for DS 2000 - Programming with Data and a Khoury Fellow for CS1200 - Leadership & Skill Development at Khoury College of Computer Sciences at Northeastern University. In addition, I am President of NU Science Magazine where I produce print content bisemesterly.
Previously, I was an Programmer/Data Analytics Intern at Schonfeld Strategic Advisors and a Performance Engineering Intern at State Street Global Advisors.

I was born and raised in southern Connecticut and now I currently reside in Boston, MA. Outside of programming, you can find me obsessing about photography (fun fact - I took the above photo of Copley Square!), reading the news, running, impromptu trips to New York City, music and thrifting. I am a classically trained pianist and am currently teaching myself how to play guitar and bass.


philosophy | personal mantra

My personal philosophy in life is to never stop in the pursuit of knowledge. Knowing more about the intricacies world and the different and unique experiences of the communities around me help me not only have a stronger grasp on the big picture mechanisms of the world, but also allows me to be a more diverse and inclusive individual and engineer. I am always looking to improve, and embrace that both hard work, success and failure are all integral parts of becoming a better version of myself every day.
