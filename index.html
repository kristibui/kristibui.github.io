about

My name is Kristi and I am a rising third year at Northeastern University currently pursuing a BS in Computer Science. I love the combination of design and problem solving associated with programming, and am passionate about promoting STEM education to underrepresented minorities and exploring the intersection of technology and civics.

Currently, I am a Head Designer for the NU Science Magazine where I design print content and manage a team of designers. In addition, I am a CCIS Fellow for CS 1200 for Fall 2018, where I teach classes and provide mentorship to first-year students entering Northeastern's College of Computer and Information Science (CCIS).
Previously, I was a Performance Engineering Intern at State Street Global Advisors.

I was born and raised in southern Connecticut and now I currently reside in Boston, MA. Outside of code, you can find me obsessing about photography, reading the news, spinning, impromptu trips to New York City and music. I am a classically trained pianist and am currently teaching myself how to play ukulele.
